%Arabica Egypt Cairo Roastery

Architects: no.10 NOMURA Co., Ltd. | Location: New Cairo City, Egypt | Year: 2024 | Area: 247 m²

Design Concept & Objectives

  • Draws inspiration from Egypt's desert landscapes and ancient ruins.
  • Concept of "piling up" with modular limestone blocks creating organic flow.

Context & Site Analysis

  • Located in U-Venues, blending modern urban development with historical context.

Spatial Organization

  • Central pathway guides customers through roasting area to seating zones.
  • Modular limestone blocks create distinct zones.

Sustainability & Innovation

  • Locally sourced limestone reduces emissions and supports local industries.
  • Emphasis on natural materials and passive cooling.

Materials & Construction

  • 800mm modular limestone blocks reflect irregular quarry landscape.
  • Homage to Egypt's natural resources and traditional construction.

Aesthetic & Form

  • Harmonious blend of traditional and contemporary aesthetics.
  • Natural limestone texture with minimalist forms for a serene atmosphere.

User Experience & Functionality

  • Immersive space reflecting Egypt's landscape.
  • Thoughtful arrangement enhances coffee-drinking experience.

Challenges & Solutions

  • Challenge: Integrating traditional materials into a modern retail space.
  • Solution: Utilizing modular limestone blocks in a contemporary arrangement.

SWOT Analysis

Strengths

Innovative use of local materials; strong cultural resonance.

Weaknesses

Potential limitations in flexibility due to the fixed nature of limestone blocks.

Opportunities

Setting a precedent for culturally inspired modern retail spaces in Egypt.

Threats

Maintenance challenges associated with natural stone materials.

%Arabica Ho Chi Minh City Roastery

Architects: Nguyen Khai Architects & Associates | Location: Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam | Year: 2024 | Area: 255 m²

Design Concept & Objectives

  • Blends rich heritage with modern aesthetics.
  • Retains traditional base materials, introduces glass blocks and rattan ceilings.

Context & Site Analysis

  • Adjacent to iconic landmarks (Central Post Office, Notre Dame Cathedral).

Spatial Organization

  • Central pathway guides customers; glass partitions divide seating areas.

Sustainability & Innovation

  • Traditional materials (rattan) and natural light contribute to sustainability.
  • Adaptive reuse minimizes environmental impact.

Materials & Construction

  • Stainless steel, exposed aggregate floors, warm wooden accents.
  • Rattan ceiling adds texture and references Vietnamese craftsmanship.

Aesthetic & Form

  • Industrial vibe softened by natural materials and light.
  • Interplay of shadows through rattan ceiling enhances sensory experience.

User Experience & Functionality

  • Indoor/outdoor seating with views of historic architecture.
  • Transparent design allows observation of coffee-making process.

Challenges & Solutions

  • Challenge: Balancing modern design with historical context preservation.
  • Solution: Integrating contemporary materials while maintaining traditional structures.

SWOT Analysis

Strengths

Strategic location; successful blend of tradition and modernity.

Weaknesses

Potential constraints due to preservation requirements.

Opportunities

Attracting both locals and tourists seeking authentic experiences.

Threats

Competition from other cafés in a densely populated area.

Anh Coffee Roastery

Architects: Red5studio | Location: Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam | Year: 2021 | Area: 374 m²

Design Concept & Objectives

  • Inspired by Vietnam's basalt red soil and cloudy skies.
  • Evokes feeling of being immersed in a floating landscape.

Context & Site Analysis

  • Central Saigon location with large façade and deep setback.

Spatial Organization

  • Connects existing columns with large communal table on first floor.
  • Pathway to outdoor garden on second floor with central atrium.

Sustainability & Innovation

  • Locally sourced materials, natural light, and ventilation.

Materials & Construction

  • Red terracotta bricks, white iron grid ceiling, navy blue accents, natural wood.

Aesthetic & Form

  • Curved communal tables and straight brick lines create dynamic environment.

User Experience & Functionality

  • Fosters community through shared spaces and integrates nature.

Challenges & Solutions

  • Challenge: Transforming a rigid office block into a warm, inviting space.
  • Solution: Utilizing terracotta bricks and a white mesh ceiling.

SWOT Analysis

Strengths

Strong brand identity; innovative use of materials.

Weaknesses

Limited brand recognition as a new entrant in the market.

Opportunities

Appealing to customers seeking unique and immersive café experiences.

Threats

Competition from established coffee brands in the area.

Klim Coffee Roasting Co.

Architects: SuiL | Location: Mount Waverley, Australia | Year: 2024 | Area: 176 m² (Internal: 136 m²; External: 35.1 m²)

Design Concept & Objectives

  • Core concept: transparency and craftsmanship, using "grid" and "box" spatial language.
  • "The Box" (glazed room) allows full visibility into roasting, turning it into a performative act.
  • Objective: Elevate coffee roasting from backend process to curated, observable experience.

Context & Site Analysis

  • Suburban Melbourne neighborhood; balances industrial functionality with café aesthetic.

Spatial Organization

  • Co-located café and roastery within tight floor plan.
  • Layout around central transparent "Roast Room," surrounded by seating, retail, kitchen.
  • Bi-folding glass doors open to timber deck for indoor-outdoor extension.

Materials & Construction

  • Timber elements for warmth; black steel framing for industrial roots.

Aesthetic & Form

  • Minimalism with raw materiality; clean but honest aesthetic.
  • Contrast between hard (glass, steel, concrete) and soft (timber, fabric) materials.

Sustainability & Innovation

  • Natural lighting and cross ventilation reduce reliance on artificial systems.
  • Optimized material selection and spatial orientation for energy efficiency.
  • Roasting process visible but thermally and acoustically isolated.

User Experience & Functionality

  • Patrons experience coffee-making in real-time due to transparent layout.
  • Open floor plan encourages movement; seating caters to individuals and groups.
  • Supports casual consumption and coffee education.

Challenges & Solutions

  • Challenge: Balancing industrial needs with cozy, high-end café aesthetic.
  • Solution: Use of "Roast Box" as visually accessible and functionally isolated environment.
  • Challenge: Space constraints in integrating multiple programs.
  • Solution: Smart zoning and multi-use central feature.

SWOT Analysis

Strengths

Clear and cohesive brand identity rooted in transparency and craftsmanship.

Weaknesses

Compact size may limit roasting capacity or customer volume during peak hours.

Opportunities

Potential to host coffee education events, tastings, or community workshops.

Threats

Higher initial investment due to specialized construction and glazing.

Possible noise or odor complaints from nearby residential areas.
